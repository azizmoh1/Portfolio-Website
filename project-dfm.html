Advanced Rapid Prototyping & DFM Case Study

In Progress | Product Design + Manufacturing

Project Overview

Designing and prototyping a novel portable tool for students, balancing functionality, ergonomics, and mass-production readiness with DFM constraints.

Problem Statement

Create a compact, interactive, support-free print design with moving/deforming features while maintaining manufacturability and low material waste.

Design Process

  • Conducted market and patent review for design differentiation.
  • Built DFM-optimized CAD assembly in SolidWorks with print-in-place tolerance planning.
  • Iterated physical prototypes and refined functional hand-feel at UIC Makerspace.

Lessons Learned

Early DFM planning significantly reduces rework in prototyping and improves design quality before full-scale manufacturing decisions.
